Blue Bird Video Player

The best way to watch videos in HTML

What is Blue Bird?

Blue Bird is a simple yet very powerfull video player for HTML just simply add 2 lines of code to get started.
Don't waste time styling and coding a video player for your blog, website, podcast, etc, just use Blue Bird.
It's fast, free & open source.

Blue Bird Video Player Documentation

The Blue Bird Video Player comes in two versions:

  • Simple Version: A basic video player with three controls – rewind 5 seconds, play/pause, and forward 5 seconds. Use the element <blue-bird-link-simple>.
  • Advanced Version: A feature-rich player that includes all simple controls plus additional features such as a progress bar, mute/unmute, and fullscreen toggle. Use the element <blue-bird-link-advanced>.

Step 1: Include the Blue Bird Video Player Script

To use the Blue Bird Video Player on your website, include the following single line in your page's <head> section: 

<https://madebydanny.uk/bluebird/api/v1.js"></script>

Make sure the https://madebydanny.uk/bluebird/api/v1.js file (which contains the code for both <blue-bird-link-simple> and <blue-bird-link-advanced>) is accessible by your site.

Step 2: Add Your Font Awesome Kit

Your page should include your Font Awesome kit in the head. If you don't have a Fontawesome kit use the one provided bellow: 

<script src="https://kit.fontawesome.com/0ca27f8db1.js" crossorigin="anonymous"></script>

Step 3: Use the Custom Element(s)

Once you have included the script and your Font Awesome kit, you can use the custom elements anywhere in your HTML.

Simple Version Usage

Insert the simple version using: 

<blue-bird-link-simple src="your-video.mp4" size="norm"></blue-bird-link-simple>

Attributes:
src – The URL (or path) to your video file.
size – (Optional) The preset size of your video player:
• big for 1080px wide,
• norm (default) for 720px wide,
• small for 480px wide.

Advanced Version Usage

Insert the advanced version using: 

<blue-bird-link-advanced src="your-video.mp4" size="norm"></blue-bird-link-advanced>

This version includes additional controls:

  • A progress bar (click to seek anywhere in the video)
  • A mute/unmute button
  • A fullscreen toggle

Complete Example

Here is a complete example of an HTML page that uses the Blue Bird Video Player elements: 

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html lang="en">
  <head>
    <meta charset="UTF-8">
    <title>My Video Page</title>
    <!-- Include Font Awesome Kit -->
    <script src="https://kit.fontawesome.com/0ca27f8db1.js" crossorigin="anonymous"></script>
    <!-- Include the Blue Bird Video Player Script -->
    <script src="https://madebydanny.uk/bluebird/api/v1.js"></script>
  </head>
  <body>
    <h1>Welcome to My Video Page</h1>
    
    <h2>Simple Video Player</h2>
    <blue-bird-link-simple src="mov_bbb.mp4" size="norm"></blue-bird-link-simple>
    
    <h2>Advanced Video Player</h2>
    <blue-bird-link-advanced src="mov_bbb.mp4" size="norm"></blue-bird-link-advanced>
  </body>
</html>

That’s it! With just one line to include the script and the use of either <blue-bird-link-simple> or <blue-bird-link-advanced>, you can easily add a custom video player to your website.
